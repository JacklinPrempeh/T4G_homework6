Our Raw Food Products

Shea Butter

Shea Butter is from the shea tree which grows naturally in the wild and the dry savannah belt of West Africa from Senegal in the west to Sudan in the east, and onto the foothills of the Ethiopian highlands. It occurs in 21 countries across the African continent, namely Benin, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Ghana, Guinea Bissau, Ivory Coast, Mali, Niger, Nigeria, Senegal, Sierra Leone, South Sudan, Sudan, Togo, Uganda, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Kenya and Guinea.Shea butter is used in cooking at home, used as hair pommades and also as cure to some skin diseases.

Cocoyam

Cocoyams are herbaceous perennial plants belonging to the family Araceae and are grown primarily for their edible roots, although all parts of the plant are edible. Cocoyam may also be referred to as taro, old cocoyam, arrowroot, eddoe, macabo, kontomire or dasheen and originates from the region of Southeast Asia.It is cooked using different methods like boiling, frying,and roasting.

African Star Apple

The African star apple (Chrysophyllum Albidum) is an indigenous fruit found in Nigeria and Ghana. It is an unusual fruit that has been proven to have several health benefits. The African star apple is known as Alasa, Alasma mba or Adasa mma in Ghana.

Basil Leaves

Basil, also called great basil, is a culinary herb of the family Lamiaceae. It is a tender plant, and is used in cuisines worldwide.Basil is most commonly used fresh in recipes. In general, it is added last, as cooking quickly destroys the flavor. The fresh herb can be kept for a short time in plastic bags in the refrigerator.You can add Basil to your stew or soup.It is also used as Insecticides and Insect Repellent.
